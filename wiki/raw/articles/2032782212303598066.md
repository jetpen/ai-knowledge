# Why 80% of Agentic AI Work Isn't AI

## Overview
Ashutosh Maheshwari explains that the "last mile" of moving agents from demo to production is largely a backend engineering challenge, not an AI challenge. 80% of the effort lies in the infrastructure required to make agents reliable, scalable, and manageable.

## The Four Infrastructure Layers
1. **Data Engineering (The Input Problem)**:
    * Reality of production inputs: messy, mixed formats (PDF, HTML, HL7, Word).
    * Requires robust normalization, cleaning, chunking, and PII scrubbing before the LLM even sees the data.
2. **State Management (The Memory Problem)**:
    * LLMs are stateless by design; agents need persistent state for multi-hour, multi-step tasks.
    * Requires working memory, episodic logs, checkpointing/snapshots, and external store integration.
3. **Retry and Recovery (The Failure Problem)**:
    * Production agents need more than simple retries because operations are often partially complete.
    * Requires idempotency keys, partial completion detection, intelligent timeout budgets, and dead-letter handling.
4. **Cost Governance (The Economics Problem)**:
    * AI costs scale poorly; demo-time costs often bear no resemblance to production usage.
    * Requires per-task cost attribution, real-time budgeting, and automated rate/usage limiting.

## Key Insight
The agent took a weekend to build; the infrastructure to make it production-ready takes a quarter. This ratio is industry standard.
