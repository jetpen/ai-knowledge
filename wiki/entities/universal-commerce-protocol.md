# Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP)

## Overview
The Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) is an open-standard, industry-driven framework facilitating "agentic commerce." It enables AI agents, commerce platforms, and merchants to interoperate across the full purchase lifecycle (discovery to post-purchase) without custom integrations.

## Core Pillars
* **Agentic Commerce:** Native support for agent-driven purchasing.
* **Merchant Sovereignty:** Merchants remain the "Merchant of Record" and maintain business rule control.
* **Interoperability:** Built on standard web patterns (REST, JSON-RPC, OAuth 2.0).
* **Cross-Protocol Integration:** Compatible with Agent Payments Protocol (AP2), Agent-to-Agent (A2A), the Model Context Protocol (MCP), and [[x402]] (HTTP-Native Payments).

## Key Components
1. **Checkout:** Supports dynamic pricing, complex cart management, and tax calculation via Native or Embedded Checkout modalities.
2. **Identity Linking:** Secure, authorized relationships through OAuth 2.0.
3. **Order Management:** Real-time visibility and post-purchase lifecycle management via webhooks.

## Ecosystem
Supported by a coalition of major retailers (e.g., Target, Walmart, Home Depot) and payment providers (e.g., Stripe, PayPal, Klarna).
